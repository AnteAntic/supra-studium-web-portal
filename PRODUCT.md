# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Croatian therapists and healthcare practitioners — massage therapists, physiotherapists, osteopaths, spa professionals — at varied experience levels seeking practical continuing education in manual therapy modalities (acupressure, trigger point therapy, manual therapy, crossfriction, cupping, Lomi Lomi). They arrive with existing clinical knowledge. They are working professionals, not hobbyists or wellness consumers.

Secondary audience: practitioners evaluating institutional credibility before committing to a course. They compare Supra Studium against other institutions, read carefully, and are put off by high-pressure or overly polished marketing.

## Product Purpose

Supra Studium is a licensed Croatian wellness therapy education institute offering in-person courses in manual therapy. It exists to train and upskill practicing therapists with clinical precision and institutional authority.

Success means: therapists recognize Supra Studium as the serious, authoritative choice in their field — and enroll, return, and recommend it to peers. The site is the institution's public face; it must convey the same professional gravity as the courses themselves.

## Brand Personality

Three words: Calm, Clinical, Editorial.

Voice: observational, precise, institutional. Not persuasive. Not urgent. Copy describes what is; it does not pitch what could be.

Emotional goals: trust earned through restraint, authority established through specificity, credibility carried by what is left out rather than what is added.

The site should feel like a private manual therapy institute, a clinical education journal, or an art-directed documentary platform — not an online course marketplace.

## References

Positive aesthetic territory:
- Swiss/German clinical institute minimalism: confident whitespace, sparse typography, no decoration
- Monocle / Kinfolk editorial restraint: art-directed photography, unhurried layout, type as atmosphere
- Documentary film / festival sites: full-bleed video, observational copy, no-sell energy
- Private educational institution energy: credentialed, understated, not trying to convince you

## Anti-references

Do not resemble or imply:
- Startup SaaS aesthetics (gradients, rounded cards, hero metrics, social proof rails)
- Wellness marketing (soft pastels, leaf motifs, "journey" language, coaching energy)
- Aggressive conversion UI (urgency badges, countdown timers, scarcity copy, price in CTAs)
- Template landing page patterns (identical card grids, icon + heading + text rows, testimonial carousels)
- Online course marketplaces (Udemy, Coursera, Mindvalley aesthetic)
- Glassmorphism or luxury funnel visual language

Forbidden phrases: transformiraj, moć dodira, instant rezultat, osiguraj mjesto odmah, ostala X mjesta, investiraj u sebe, premium, ekskluzivno, breakthrough.

## Design Principles

1. **Restraint as authority.** Every element earns its place through what it removes, not adds. Decoration signals insecurity. Absence signals confidence.

2. **Clinical precision over persuasion.** Specific anatomical language (palpacija, reakcija tkiva, napeta traka, trigger točka, ishemijski pritisak, PIR) builds more trust than superlatives. Show the curriculum; don't sell the outcome.

3. **Documentary observation over sales narrative.** The site describes; it does not pitch. Copy reads like a case note or a journal entry, not a course landing page.

4. **Institutional rhythm.** Spacing, type hierarchy, and layout carry the weight of credibility. A 20px margin decision is a brand decision. Monotony in spacing is a credibility failure.

5. **Respect professional intelligence.** The user is a trained therapist. The site treats them as a peer, not a prospect. No hand-holding, no urgency, no over-explanation.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target: WCAG 2.1 AA.

Primary audience reads Croatian. No multilingual support currently required. Ensure sufficient contrast for body text on ivory and video-overlay contexts. Respect reduced-motion preferences for video autoplay and scroll animations.
